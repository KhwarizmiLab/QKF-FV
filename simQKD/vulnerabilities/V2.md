
### **V2: Identity Misbinding**

**Objective:** Execute a Man-in-the-Middle (MitM) attack by manipulating classical Message Authentication Codes (MACs) while blindly forwarding quantum states, forcing Alice and Bob to establish a key with each other while believing they share it with the attacker.

**Testbed Replication Steps:**

1. **Establish Concurrent Sessions:** The attacker ($Charlie$) initiates two independent classical QKD sessions: one with Alice (acting as Responder) using pre-shared key $psk_{AC}$, and one with Bob (acting as Initiator) using $psk_{CB}$.
2. **Blind Qubit Relay:** Receive the quantum transmission ($q_A$) from Alice and immediately forward it to Bob untouched. Do not measure or alter the qubits.
3. **Intercept Classical Messages:** Intercept the classical messages (basis announcements, sifting data) sent between Alice and Bob.
4. **Swap MAC Tags:** * When receiving a message from Alice authenticated with $psk_{AC}$, strip the MAC tag.
* Generate a new MAC tag for the payload using $psk_{CB}$ and forward it to Bob.
* Repeat this process in reverse for messages originating from Bob.


5. **Bypass Eavesdropper Detection:** Because the quantum states were undisturbed, parameter estimation (error rate checks) will succeed.
6. **Observe Misbinding:** Allow the protocol to complete. Alice and Bob will successfully derive a shared key ($k_{AB}$). Alice will incorrectly bind this key to $Charlie$'s identity, and Bob will also incorrectly bind it to $Charlie$'s identity, successfully bypassing entity authentication.

### **V4: Message Reflection**

**Objective:** Bypass entity authentication by reflecting a participant's own MAC-authenticated messages back to them, tricking them into finalizing a session key based on attacker-injected qubits.

**Testbed Replication Steps:**

1. **Inject Malicious Qubits:** The attacker ($Eve^+$) generates an encoded qubit stream ($q_{Eve}$) derived from a known raw key ($k_{Eve}$). Transmit $q_{Eve}$ to the target (Bob) over the quantum channel.
2. **Trigger Parameter Estimation:** Wait for Bob to measure the qubits and initiate the parameter estimation phase by transmitting his sampled key ($k_{B}^{sampled}$) over the classical channel.
3. **Intercept and Reflect:** Intercept Bob's classical message containing $k_{B}^{sampled}$ and its associated MAC tag. Immediately reflect the exact, unmodified message and MAC tag back to Bob.
4. **Exploit Missing Directionality:** Bob receives the reflected message. Because the protocol's MAC implementation lacks strict contextual binding (e.g., directionality flags or nonces), Bob successfully authenticates his own MAC tag.
5. **Force 0% Error Rate:** Bob compares his locally sampled key against the reflected sampled key. They match perfectly, yielding a 0% error rate.
6. **Complete Session:** Bob accepts the parameter estimation and generates a session key based on $Eve^+$'s qubits, falsely believing he has authenticated and established a secure session with a legitimate peer (Alice).

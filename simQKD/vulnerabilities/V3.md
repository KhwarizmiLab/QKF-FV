
### **V3: Basis-Deferred Measurement**

**Objective:** Exploit Prepare-and-Measure (PM) QKD implementations that allow the sender (Alice) to announce her measurement basis before the receiver (Bob) has measured the qubits.

**Testbed Replication Steps:**

1. **Configure Protocol Ordering:** Ensure the testbed PM QKD protocol is configured such that the basis disclosure step happens *before* the receiver finishes qubit detection (called sequence A).
2. **Intercept and Store:** Intercept Alice's transmitted qubit stream ($q_A$) on the quantum channel. Store these qubits in quantum memory without measuring them.
3. **Wait for Basis Announcement:** Monitor the classical channel until Alice announces her measurement basis ($b_A$).
4. **Measure with Known Basis:** Retrieve $q_A$ from quantum memory and measure it using Alice's publicly announced basis ($b_A$). This perfectly extracts Alice's raw key ($k_{A}^{raw}$).
5. **Forge and Forward:** Using the extracted $k_{A}^{raw}$ and basis $b_A$, forge a new sequence of qubits ($q'_A$) and transmit them to Bob over the quantum channel.
6. **Relay Classical Data:** Passively relay Alice's basis ($b_A$) to Bob, and Bob's basis ($b_B$) to Alice during the sifting phase.
7. **Reconstruct Key:** Because the forged qubits perfectly match Alice's original states, parameter estimation will pass. Eavesdrop on the public reconciliation data and apply it to $k_{A}^{raw}$ to reconstruct the final shared key ($k_{AB}$).

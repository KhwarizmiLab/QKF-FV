### **V1: Subverted Entanglement Injection**

**Objective:** Exploit the lack of timing synchronization in Entanglement-Based (EB) QKD to extract the session key by delaying one participant's qubits until the other's measurement basis is announced.

**Testbed Replication Steps:**

1. **Intercept the Source:** Position the attacker ($Eve^+$) to control the entangled qubit source meant for Alice and Bob.
2. **Generate and Split:** Generate a sequence of entangled qubit pairs. Split them into two streams: $q_A$ (intended for Alice) and $q_B$ (intended for Bob).
3. **Delay Transmission:** Transmit $q_B$ to Bob over the quantum channel, but retain $q_A$ inside a quantum memory module.
4. **Monitor Classical Channel:** Wait for Bob to measure his qubits and announce his randomly chosen measurement basis, $b_B$, over the public classical channel.
5. **Correlated Measurement:** Once $b_B$ is announced, measure the retained qubits ($q_A$) using Bob's exact basis ($b_B$). Due to quantum entanglement, this yields a raw key ($k_{B}^{'raw}$) that is perfectly correlated/identical to Bob's raw key.
6. **Forge and Transmit:** Use the extracted raw key ($k_{B}^{'raw}$) and Bob's basis ($b_B$) to encode a new, forged sequence of qubits ($q'_A$). Transmit $q'_A$ to Alice.
7. **Pass Parameter Estimation:** Passively forward all subsequent classical basis announcements and sampled keys between Alice and Bob. Because the raw keys match, the CHSH inequality checks will pass without flagging an eavesdropper.
8. **Reconstruct Key:** Apply the publicly exchanged error reconciliation and privacy amplification data to $k_{B}^{'raw}$ to independently derive the final symmetric session key, $k_{AB}$.
